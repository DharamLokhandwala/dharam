Madhi Ni Khamni

Madhi Ni khamni is a breakfast company established more than 75 years ago in the village of Madhi(Gujarat, India). Their famous food item is Khamni, which is being loved till today. Currently their family business has more than 10 branches in Surat(Gujarat, India).

My Role

Research, Ideation, Wireframing, Visual Designing, Development

Deliverables

The whole website(www.madhinikhamni.com)

Client

Mr Hari Khamniwala

Sector

Food products

Context and challenges

Problems

As mentioned earlier, the company and their products are very trusted and loved in Surat(Gujarat, India) but outside Gujarat, people are unaware of his company and products. So the main concern of Mr. Khamniwala was to spread brand awareness and a sense of trust through the website.

One other reason to approach me was to advertise their recent and most ambitious product “Ready to eat khamni” which is being exported to the USA. Mr Khamniwala wanted to showcase their newly started product via the website.

Project goals
  1. To take their offline business online
  2. To spread brand awareness and build Trust
  3. Showcase the newly started product “Ready to eat Khamni” and increase it’s sale
  4. Increase their product sales by showcasing their main products to people outside Gujarat(India)

Process

Researching
Creating Sitemap

Development

Researching

Madhi Ni khamni has more than 7 products which is the ideal number of things a person can keep in mind. So the main goal of this stage was to list out all the products and all the possible pages needed for the website and their arrangement and categorisation was done in the next site-mapping stage.

I personally knew the brand very well so I did not have to dive deep to get to know the products well. Competitor analysis was also carried out at this stage.

Sitemap

I got the raw requirements from Mr Khamniwala via online meeting and also few requirements from competitor analysis. Now was the time to categorize these requirements and give a logical non-visual structure to the website.

Site-map was presented and approved by Mr Khamniwala before moving to the next stage of wireframing.

Wireframing

Once the site map was approved, I had a non visual structure of the website. So the next step was to create a low fidelity structure of the website which acted as a skeleton.

skip to visual designing

Visual Designing

Here in the Hero Section, the main aim was to show their featured products and have a call to action button for their products. Therefore I made a custom background consisting of their featured products with their Tagline.

One of the project goal was to build trust, so what brings trust among people? Years old family business with a name. People tend to buy from companies that were established years ago than startups. One other thing that brings trust are the reviews. So, I added a reviews section on landing page.

view final mockups

User Testing

Testing with real users is a very important step for building better user experiences. Usability Test was designed to test the expected and actual user flows. Fortunately, the website was not much complex and thus the results of Usability Testing was in my favour and the user flows were as expected
